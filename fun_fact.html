The Dark Side of The Moon

The far side of the Moon, known to the public as "The Dark Side of the Moon," is the lunar hemisphere that always faces away from Earth.

But why❓

The Moon always shows the same face to Earth because it is tidally locked, a result of gravitational forces acting over millions of years. This happened due to an imbalance between the Moon's center of mass and its center of gravity, with the near side containing denser materials. Earth's gravity pulled on this mass, gradually slowing the Moon’s rotation until it matched its orbit. As a result, one side remains hidden from view — often called the "dark side," though both sides receive sunlight.
